# Limited Precision Principle (LPP)

**Author:** Vladimir Shestopalov  
**First public disclosure:** April 29, 2026  
**Version:** v1.0

## Abstract

This paper formulates the **Limited Precision Principle (LPP)**, which postulates that physically realizable dynamics cannot require more information to determine observable outcomes than can, in principle, be physically extracted from the system itself. The principle is motivated by a conceptual tension between the mathematical use of real numbers with formally infinite precision and established physical limits on information capacity, including bounds associated with gravitational thermodynamics.

LPP does not constrain mathematics as such, but restricts which mathematical dependencies may correspond to physical law: observable behavior cannot depend on arbitrarily remote digits of parameters that are fundamentally inaccessible. Under this framework, only information that is physically available may influence measurable evolution.

The paper examines the compatibility of this principle with fine-tuning arguments, quantum mechanics, and gravitational singularities, and proposes that LPP may manifest experimentally as a universal irreducible precision floor in physical evolution — including residual decoherence, limits to scalable entanglement, fundamental phase noise, and imperfect reversibility even under ideal control. This transforms LPP from a conceptual principle into a falsifiable physical hypothesis concerning the informational limits of nature.
