# The Limited Precision Principle: On the Informational Limits of Physical Law

**Author:** Vladimir Shestopalov

## Abstract


## 1. Introduction
Modern physics is formulated using mathematical structures that rely fundamentally on real numbers, whose informational content is formally infinite. In practice, however, physical systems appear subject to finite informational constraints. Bounds associated with thermodynamics, gravitation, and quantum measurement suggest that the amount of physically accessible information in any finite system is limited. This creates a conceptual tension: if nature is informationally finite, can observable physical evolution legitimately depend on infinitely precise numerical distinctions?

This tension motivates a simple but potentially fundamental constraint: observable physical evolution should not depend on information that is inaccessible in principle. Mathematical descriptions may employ idealized continuous variables and arbitrarily precise numerical values, but physical law itself should only be sensitive to distinctions that can, at least in principle, be physically represented, extracted, or operationally realized within the system.
